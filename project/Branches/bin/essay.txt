
Through both Quinn and Junior’s decision to do what is out of the ordinary for justice in All American Boys and The Absolutely True Diary of a Part-Time Indian, 
after being inspired by someone, and both Quinn and Junior facing opposition from their friends, all of the authors show that taking a stand for justice, 
whether it be for themselves or for another demographic, usually starts with someone else’s inspiration, and usually comes with opposition from friends. 
From Jill and Mr. P motivating Quinn and Junior respectively into standing for justice, all authors convey that taking a stand will usually start with 
motivation from someone else. In All American Boys, Quinn is unsure about whether he should be on Paul’s side because his best friend, Guzzo, is Paul’s brother,
or if he should be on Rashad’s side, for what is right. Jill convinces Quinn that everyone saying “Not my Problem” and that “It’s a One time thing” 
fails to recognize that “Every time it happened”, showing the people are willing to ignore examples of injustice by disregarding it(Reynolds, Jason and Brendan Kiely 182). 
This results in Quinn recognizing that everytime he ignores these events he is essentially standing on the side of the perpetrator. 
Quinn then recognizes through his little brother, being “deep in his own world”, that he “[had] been doing the same damn thing the last couple days”, 
showing that he is starting to care about this situation and try to remove his ignorance(Reynolds, Jason and Brendan Kiely 183). 
He lastly remarks that “I didn’t want to walk away anymore”, showing that through Jill’s motivation, Quinn has chosen to be an upstander (Reynolds, Jason and Brendan Kiely 183). 
Mr. P is like Jill in that he also motivates the main character into standing up for what’s right, however, the circumstances are a lot darker. 
He motivates Junior to leave the reservation, as the schools there are actively trying to demotivate them. He sees Junior’s defiance when he throws the book at him, 
and urges him to leave the reservation, as he says “We’re all going to kill you”(Alexie 43). 
This quote does not explicitly mean that all of the people on the reservation are going to kill Junior, 
rather demotivate him, and ultimately continue the cycle of poverty. Mr. P sees that, and wants Junior to “take your[Junior’s] hope and go somewhere”, 
so that Junior could break the cycle. Junior ultimately decides to go to reardan due to Mr. P’s motivation. Through these examples of standing up, 
all authors convey that taking a stand for what is right often requires some source of inspiration from others. 
Taking a stand for what is right can often result in your friends and people you know opposing you. In The Absolutely True Diary of a Part-Time Indian,
 We see that many Indians, including Junior’s Best friend, Rowdy, have turned against him due to him going to Reardan, in which they regarded Junior as a traitor. 
 One example of this is when Junior goes trick-or-treating on the reservation for money to donate to the poor. However, as he is going home,
he gets attacked by 4 people wearing Frankenstein masks. He was “shoved to the ground” and “kicked a few times” showing the hostility of the people from the reservation, 
and was “spit on”, showing how much some people hated him (Alexie 79). The spit also “made me[Junior] from feeling like an insect”, 
showing how the people intended to dehumanize Junior, and shows the extreme lengths people will go after someone decides to stand up for himself (Alexie 79). 
In All American Boys, Quinn faces Similar opposition from His Best friend, Guzzo. As people take the side of Rashad, 
Guzzo becomes increasingly Angry and Paranoid that everyone hates him due to Paul. In Basketball Practice, Tooms knocks Guzzo down with an elbow, 
and Guzzo is convinced that Tooms did it on purpose. After Quinn decided to be on Rashad’s side, Guzzo became increasingly aggressive towards Quinn. 
He “knows what side you're[Quinn’s] on”, showing that he obviously now hates Quinn a little due to the side he took (Reynolds, Jason and Brendan Kiely 223). 
Another example of this is that later in the book, Quinn decides to bring a shirt that clearly motivates others to protest. Guzzo saw this, 
and waited after Basketball Practice was over to attack him. Guzzo saying “Don’t let me see your face in our house again” is implying that Guzzo has no longer 
considered Quinn a friend, due to his stand for Justice. Through the examples above, 
all authors convey that there are some friends that will oppose you when you take a stand for what’s right.
Through Quinn’s experiences with Jill and Guzzo, and Junior’s experiences with Mr. P and his reservation’s people, all of the authors convey that people, 
including your friends, can either inspire you or oppose you when you stand up for what’s right. Your friends can motivate you into taking a stand, 
by showing the true colors of something. However, they can also stop you, purely due to personal interests, or relations to the perpetrator. 
The 2 books both tell the same story about standing up for people, but the 2 books vary in humor and the severity of the situation. 
They both show the reality of people standing up for themselves, and also show that conditions do not matter when standing up for people.
